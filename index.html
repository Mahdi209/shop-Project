Types of Pencils

Graphite Pencils
Invented in 1564 following a significant graphite deposit discovery in England, graphite pencils have become a staple in everyday writing, sketching, and drawing. These pencils are graded using the B and H scale, where B (soft) leads create darker lines and H (hard) leads create lighter lines.
$16.99
Watercolor Pencils
Watercolor pencils bring together the precision of colored pencils and the fluidity of watercolor paint. These pencils, equipped with water-soluble pigments, offer the unique ability to blend, layer, and create gradients of color. They are a popular choice among illustrators and graphic designers for their versatility in dry and wet forms.
$19.99
Colored Pencils
Tracing their roots back to Johann von Goethe in the 1820s and officially invented by Faber-Castell in 1908, colored pencils have revolutionized the world of art and crafting. These pencils, made from a blend of wax or oil-based binders and pigments, offer a wide range of color options and blending possibilities, making them ideal for both artistic endeavors and practical tasks.
